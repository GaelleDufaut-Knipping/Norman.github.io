The Design of Everyday Things

Originally published in 1988 under the title"The Psychology of Everyday Things", "The Design of Everyday Things‚Äù deals with the relationship between design and the interaction between an object and its user. It looks at ways of improving this interaction so that using the object becomes a pleasurable experience. In this book, Norman introduces the concept of affordance in design, drawing on James J. Gibson's ideas of ecological psychology. Affordances in design are illustrated by elements such as flat plates on doors that signal that they should be pushed, small finger-friendly push-buttons and elongated, rounded bars instinctively used as handles.

Emotional Design


"Emotional Design" is both the title of a book by Donald Norman and the central concept he presents. The book deals with the importance of emotions in human cognition and learning processes. It explores how emotions play a central role in the way individuals apprehend the world and acquire new knowledge. Research indicates that emotions have a significant impact on how people process information and make decisions. For example, aesthetically pleasing objects tend to seem more effective to users because of their sensual appeal.

The Design of Everyday Things

Don Norman outlines 3 ways design can make you happy. In this 2003 talk, design critic Don Norman turns his incisive eye to beauty, fun, pleasure and emotion, as he looks at design that makes people happy. He defines the three emotional reactions that a well-designed product must to be successful.
